# International Travel Policy

*From the Office of Chief Information Officer, State of Minnesota*

**Version:** 1.1  
**Effective:** 2/1/2024  
**Revised:** 12/31/2024  
**Approved:** John Israel, State Chief Information Security Officer — Date: 3/20/2025

---

## Policy Statement

The State of Minnesota must ensure the security of assets and data while users are traveling outside the United States and that proper authorization is granted to users who wish to access state systems and data while traveling abroad; accordingly, only devices approved by MNIT for international travel shall be used, and approval to take these devices and access state systems and data must be obtained prior to travel. MNIT has the ultimate authority to approve or deny access based on the risk those locations present.

Employees must abide by the terms of this policy. Employees who violate this policy may be subject to discipline, up to and including discharge.

---

## Reason for the Policy

Conducting State business outside of the United States presents unique risks in addition to the ones present when conducting business within the United States. Setting consistent requirements for how business is conducted while outside the country allows the State to better protect its systems and data.

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

### Employees, Contractors, and Vendors

- Be aware of and follow this policy and related standards and procedures.
- Contact information security staff or email GRC@state.mn.us with questions about this policy and related standards and procedures.

### Supervisors and Managers

- Ensure employees, contractors, and vendors are proficient in this policy and related standards and procedures that are relevant to their role.
- Hold employees, contractors, and vendors accountable for following this policy and related standards and procedures.

### MNIT Governance, Risk, and Compliance

- Develop, maintain, and assess compliance with this policy.
- Provide training on this policy.
- Assist agencies and personnel with understanding and implementing this policy and related standards and procedures.

---

## Applicability

This policy applies to all departments, agencies, offices, councils, boards, commissions, and other entities in the executive branch of Minnesota State Government. This policy only applies during international travel up to 30 days. International access more than 30 days must follow the remote work or telework policy.
